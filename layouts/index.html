About me

I am an M.S. student in Statistics at the University of California, Davis. Before coming to UC Davis, I received my B.S. degree in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from Shanghai Jiao Tong University from 2021 to 2025.

My academic interests are broadly centered on mathematical statistics, machine learning theory, and geometric methods in data science. I am particularly interested in high-dimensional statistics, optimal transport, functional analysis, differential geometry, and optimization dynamics.

I am currently exploring problems related to edge-of-stability dynamics in gradient descent, transport dynamics in Wasserstein space, and the theoretical foundations of modern machine learning algorithms.

Research Project

Limit Behavior of Optimal Transport

Studied the asymptotic dynamics of iterated optimal transport maps in Wasserstein space using tools from optimal transport theory and Otto calculus. In one dimension, characterized limiting fixed points through intersections of cumulative distribution functions and analyzed their stability properties. In higher dimensions, investigated stability behavior of transport fixed points through the Monge–Ampère equation, and verified the theory through Sinkhorn-based numerical simulations on bounded domains.
